## Passage

Know Your Customer (KYC) norms require banks to verify the identity and address of every account holder. This helps prevent money laundering and terrorist financing. Customers must submit valid documents such as Aadhaar, PAN, or passport. Banks also monitor unusual transactions and report suspicious activity to the Financial Intelligence Unit. Non-compliance can lead to heavy penalties for banks. While KYC may feel inconvenient to customers, it protects the financial system and honest account holders. Digital KYC using video verification is now allowed in many cases, making onboarding faster without weakening security.
